The Shadow War

Unpacking Sri Lanka's Drug Crisis Through Arrests, Seizures, and Treatment

By: Chatura Dissanayake, Information Designer

0%

An Overlooked Reality

Sri Lanka faces a persistent and complex drug crisis. While law enforcement has been at the forefront, official data reveals a deeper, often overlooked reality. This analysis examines the past six years of data to provide a clearer picture of the nation's struggle, revealing a critical imbalance between enforcement and rehabilitation.

The Political Context of a Crisis

The battle against drug abuse has evolved under successive political administrations. An analysis of arrest and seizure data across presidential periods reveals distinct trends, reflecting shifts in policy. While each administration pledged to combat the drug trade, the numbers show moments of heightened crackdown alongside periods of consistent struggle, shaping the long-term trajectory of the crisis.

The Escalating Scale of Seizures

The sheer volume of narcotics seized paints a stark picture. From 2018 to 2023, authorities intercepted thousands of kilograms of illicit substances, with cannabis and heroin consistently dominating. These figures highlight successful police operations but also suggest a robust supply chain that continues to feed the market despite aggressive interdiction efforts.

A Swell in Arrests

As seizures grew, so did drug-related arrests, pointing to an intensified crackdown. However, a closer look reveals that a significant portion of those arrested are not kingpins but individuals with substance use disorders. This distinction is crucial, as it shifts the focus from a purely criminal justice issue to one with significant public health implications.

The State of Rehabilitation

The landscape of rehabilitation providers highlights limited treatment capacity. The scale of the crisis far outstrips the resources of government and non-governmental rehabilitation centers, which struggle to meet the growing demand. This lack of available resources means that for many, a path to recovery remains out of reach.

A Geography of the Crisis

The drug crisis is a nationwide challenge, not confined to one area. A district-by-district breakdown reveals the geographical spread of drug-related activities, pinpointing where the problem is most prevalent. From major metropolitan areas to rural regions, the map of drug findings underscores the extensive reach of this issue.
